# Adidas: The Three Stripes Legacy

## Origin Story (1920-1949)
Adidas was founded by Adolf "Adi" Dassler in 1949 in Herzogenaurach, Germany. The name "Adidas" is a combination of Adi's first and last names. Before founding Adidas, Adi and his brother Rudolf Dassler operated the Dassler Brothers Shoe Factory (1924-1948). The brothers famously split in 1948, with Rudolf going on to found Puma.

## Key Founding Story
Adi Dassler was a passionate athlete and craftsman who created the first spiked running shoes in 1925. He believed that athletes needed equipment that would help them perform better. This philosophy drove the company's innovation culture.

## Iconic Brand Identity

### The Three Stripes
The famous Three Stripes were introduced in 1967 and represent the company's commitment to versatility and performance. The stripes originally provided additional support to the shoe structure and became Adidas's trademark visual identity.

### The Trefoil Logo
The three-leaf trefoil logo was introduced in 1972. Each leaf represents one of Adidas's three core values:
- **Performance**: Athletic excellence
- **Sports**: Competition and achievement
- **Culture**: Lifestyle and fashion

## Revolutionary Technologies

### Boost Technology (2013-Present)
Boost was developed in partnership with BASF, using unique foam pellets:
- **Energy Return**: 30% more energy return than standard EVA foam
- **Temperature Resistance**: Performs consistently in various conditions
- **Cushioning**: Combines softness and responsiveness
- **Key Models**: Ultraboost, Yeezy Boost, PureBoost

### Primeknit (2013-Present)
Adidas's response to Nike's Flyknit, using seamless, knitted construction:
- **Lightweight**: Reduces material waste
- **Adaptive Fit**: Molds to the foot
- **Breathable**: Better air circulation
- **Sustainable**: Less production waste

### 4D Technology (2017-Present)
Digital Light Synthesis (3D-printed soles):
- **Computational Design**: Data-driven lattice structure
- **Customization**: Potential for personalized performance
- **Future Technology**: Represents next-generation manufacturing

### Lightstrike (2019-Present)
A lightweight, responsive cushioning foam:
- **Lighter Than Boost**: 40% lighter
- **Responsive**: Quick energy return
- **Stable**: Good for basketball and running
- **Key Models**: Harden, Dame, and running shoes

## Cultural Impact

### The Kanye West Collaboration
In 2013, Kanye West joined Adidas from Nike:
- **Yeezy Boost 750 (2015)**: First Yeezy at Adidas
- **Yeezy Boost 350 (2015)**: Became a cultural phenomenon
- **Yeezy Effect**: Created unprecedented hype and resale value
- **Yeezy Season**: Expanded into fashion shows and clothing
- **Long-term Partnership**: One of the most successful artist collaborations

### Other Major Collaborations
- **Pharrell Williams**: Human Race collection
- **Beyoncé**: Ivy Park
- **Jeremy Scott**: Artistic and whimsical designs
- **Rick Owens**: Avant-garde fashion
- **Palace**: Skate culture collaboration

## Sports Partnerships
Adidas has deep roots in sport:
- **Soccer**: Official World Cup supplier
- **NBA**: Official uniform supplier 2017-2020
- **NFL**: Performance apparel
- **Olympics**: Sponsorship of major athletes

## Sustainability
Adidas's sustainability commitments:
- **Parley for the Oceans**: Using recycled ocean plastic
- **End Plastic Waste**: Goal of 100% recycled polyester by 2025
- **Closed Loop**: Making shoes that can be recycled
- **Sustainability Innovation**: Creating products with minimal environmental impact
