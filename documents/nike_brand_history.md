# Nike: The Swoosh Revolution

## Foundation and Early Years (1964-1970)
Nike was founded in 1964 as Blue Ribbon Sports by Bill Bowerman (University of Oregon track coach) and Phil Knight (a middle-distance runner). The company started as a distributor for Onitsuka Tiger (now ASICS) running shoes before developing their own footwear. In 1971, the company officially became Nike, Inc., named after the Greek goddess of victory.

## The Swoosh Logo Design
The iconic Swoosh logo was designed in 1971 by Carolyn Davidson, a graphic design student at Portland State University. She was paid $35 for her design work. The Swoosh represents the wing of Nike, the Greek goddess of victory. This simple but powerful design has become one of the most recognizable corporate symbols in the world.

## Innovation and Technology

### Nike Air Technology (1978-Present)
Nike Air technology was developed by aerospace engineer Frank Rudy. The system uses pressurized air-filled units encapsulated in polyurethane to provide lightweight, responsive cushioning. Key innovations include:
- **Air Tailwind (1978)**: First shoe with Air technology
- **Visible Air (1987)**: Air Max 1 revealed the air unit
- **Zoom Air (1995)**: Low-profile, responsive cushioning
- **Air Max 360 (2006)**: Full-length visible Air
- **VaporMax (2017)**: Air unit exposed and used as the entire sole

### Revolutionary Materials
Nike has pioneered numerous materials that changed footwear:
- **Flyknit (2012)**: Engineered yarn fabric for lightweight support
- **Flywire (2008)**: Ultra-lightweight strength and support
- **Adapt Technology (2019)**: Self-lacing system with app control
- **Space Hippie (2020)**: Made from recycled materials
- **Next% Foam**: Advanced running shoe cushioning

## Cultural Impact

### The Michael Jordan Partnership
In 1984, Nike signed rookie basketball player Michael Jordan to a five-year, $2.5 million deal. This partnership revolutionized the sneaker industry:
- **Air Jordan 1 (1985)**: Banned by the NBA, making them instantly iconic
- **Air Jordan Line**: One of the most successful athlete endorsements ever
- **Jordan Brand**: Became a separate division in 1997
- The partnership has generated billions in revenue

## Sustainability Initiatives
Nike has committed to reducing environmental impact through:
- **Move to Zero**: Zero carbon and zero waste initiative
- **Recycled materials**: Using recycled polyester and rubber
- **Nike Grind**: Recycling old shoes into new products
- **Circular design**: Creating products designed for recycling

- **1988**: "Just Do It" campaign launched

- **2003**: Acquisition of Converse
- **2012**: Flyknit technology introduced
- **2020**: Revenue exceeds $37 billion
