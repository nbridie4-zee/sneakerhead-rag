# Puma: The Fastest Cat

## Founding Story (1948)
Puma was founded in 1948 by Rudolf Dassler, following his split from brother Adi Dassler. The company was originally named Ruda (Rudolf Dassler) but was renamed Puma in 1949. The iconic leaping cat logo was created to represent speed, power, and agility.

## The Dassler Brothers Split
The split between Adi and Rudolf Dassler was the result of personal and business conflicts. After World War II, the brothers divided their company:
- **Adi Dassler**: Founded Adidas
- **Rudolf Dassler**: Founded Puma
This split created two of the world's largest sportswear companies in the same small German town, causing fierce competition and family drama that lasted decades.

## Puma Technology

### RS (Running System) Technology
RS technology has been Puma's signature cushioning system:
- **Original RS-100 (1986)**: First RS model for running
- **RS-350**: Advanced cushioning for long distance
- **RS-X (Modern)**: Retro-inspired lifestyle design
- **RS-Z**: Comfort and style combination

### TRINOMIC Technology
A unique cushioning system using hexagonal cells:
- **Energy Transfer**: Cells compress and return energy
- **Stability**: Excellent support and balance
- **Durability**: Long-lasting performance
- **Key Models**: Mosto, LQD CELL

### NRGY Technology
Puma's answer to Boost foam:
- **Energy Return**: Similar to Adidas Boost
- **Comfort**: Excellent underfoot feel
- **Lightweight**: Comparable to other premium foams
- **Key Models**: Experience NRGY

### LQD CELL Technology
A new generation of cushioning:
- **Liquid Foam**: Innovative polymer material
- **Responsive**: Quick energy return
- **Durable**: Longer lifespan than traditional foam
- **Key Models**: LQD CELL Origin, LQD CELL Method

## Iconic Designs

### Puma Suede (1968)
One of Puma's most iconic shoes:
- **Simple Design**: Classic sneaker silhouette
- **Versatile**: Can be worn for sports or lifestyle
- **Longevity**: Still in production after 50+ years
- **Cultural Icon**: Associated with breakdance and hip-hop

### Puma Clyde (1973)
Named after basketball player Walt "Clyde" Frazier:
- **Suede Upper**: Premium materials
- **Classic Style**: Retro basketball design
- **Cultural Significance**: Basketball and fashion icon
- **Modern Adaptations**: Continually re-released

### Puma RS-100 (1986)
The original RS model:
- **Revolutionary**: Advanced running shoe
- **Technology**: Early RS cushioning
- **Design**: Bright colors and bold aesthetics
- **Influence**: Set the stage for future RS models

### Puma RS-X (2018-Present)
Modern chunky lifestyle sneaker:
- **Retro Design**: Inspired by 80s/90s running shoes
- **Bold Colors**: Extreme color combinations
- **Chunky Silhouette**: Currently trending design
- **Comfort**: Modern RS cushioning technology
